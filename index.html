The Four Color Theorem

The Four Color Theorem states that any map can be colored using just four colors in such a way that no two adjacent regions have the same color. It was first proposed in the 19th century and was finally proven in 1976 with the aid of computers. The theorem has implications for planar graphs and reduces the complexity of certain optimization problems. While extensions and generalizations of the theorem have been explored, the original statement remains a captivating mathematical problem. The Four Color Theorem represents an intersection of mathematics, computer science, and graph theory and showcases the power of mathematical abstraction and computer-assisted proofs.
